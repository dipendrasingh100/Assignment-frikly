Laminates & Sunmica

Laminates and Sunmica are prime materials used in interior design and furniture manufacturing. These are thin, multi-layered overlays that are used to provide a decorative and durable surface for furniture, cabinets, countertops, and other interior surfaces. The top layer of laminates and mica includes printed decorative paper, and the bottom layers are typically kraft paper! These layered materials are preferred for their aesthetic appeal, durability, and easy maintenance. You can select laminates and sunmica from a wide range of designs, colors, textures, and patterns that allow for versatility in design choices!

PVC Laminates

PVC laminates are a decorative surfacing material that is super popular in interior design and furniture industries. Crafted from polyvinyl chloride (PVC) resin, these sheets are used to cover various surfaces like furniture, cabinets, countertops, and wall panels, to improve their appearance and durability. PVC laminates are water-resistant, versatile, and cost-effective solutions to improve the aesthetics of interiors!

Acrylic laminates

Acrylic laminates are a trendy surfacing material used for various interior applications like design and construction. Acrylic sheets or acrylic panels are synthetic polymers, made from polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA), which is a transparent thermoplastic. Acrylic laminates are a solid, durable, and versatile material and can be selected from various colors, patterns, and finishes. Acrylic sheets are lightweight, scratch-resistant, UV and impact resistant, and are very easy to maintain!

Inner Mica

Sunmica or mica is a commonly used term for laminated decorative veneer sheets, especially in India and some other South Asian countries. Mica laminates are decorative materials that are widely used for interior decoration and furniture surfaces. Inner mica sheets are an attractive material that is added to the inner parts/sides of furniture, cabinets, shelves, tabletops, doors, and more for protection and aesthetic purposes!

Laminates & Sunmica By Types

Laminates are available in various types, each with its unique features and uses. Some of the common types of laminates are:

High-Pressure Laminate (HPL)
High-pressure laminate is one of the most popular types of laminates which are crafted by stacking multiple layers of kraft paper infused with phenolic resin and further covered with a decorative layer. The layered Kraft stack is then compressed under high heat and pressure. HPL laminates are chosen for their durability, scratch resistance, and impact resistance and are mostly preferred for making it suitable for high-traffic areas and commercial applications.

Low-Pressure Laminate (LPL)
Low-pressure laminates are manufactured at lower pressures and temperatures. It comprised multiple layers of kraft paper infused with melamine resin and a decorative layer as a surface. LPL is added to furniture surfaces and other applications where high durability is not a prime concern.

Post-Forming Laminate
Post-forming laminate is a type of High-Pressure Laminate or Low-Pressure Laminate that can be moulded or transformed into curved shapes. These laminate types are chosen to craft rounded, curvaceous edges and structures on countertops and other furniture pieces.

Digital Print Laminate
Digital print laminates allow for custom prints, designs, patterns and images to be digitally printed on the decorative layer and provide creative freedom and flexibility in design choices.

Phenolic Backed Laminate
The phenolic-backed laminate includes a phenolic resin layer on the back that aids in additional strength and moisture resistance to surfaces. These laminates are mostly used for applications where moisture exposure is common and inevitable, such as restroom partitions, countertops, tables, and more.

Metal Laminate
Metal laminates include thin sheets of metals, like aluminum or stainless steel, that are bonded to a backing material like HPL. Metal laminates give a metallic appearance and are majorly preferred for decorative accents and surfaces.

Wood Veneer Laminate
Wood veneer laminates are a thin layer of natural wood veneer bonded to a substrate material like plywood or particle board. This laminate provides the aesthetics of real wood in a cost-effective way.

Marble and Stone Laminates
Marble and stone laminate is a type of laminate that imitates the look of marble or natural stone. These trendy laminate sheets or panels have a visual representation of marble or stone patterns on its surface and provide the luxurious feel and appeal to the walls, furniture, countertops, and more!

Plain Solid Color Laminates
Solid color laminate is a common laminate material used in interior design and construction. Unlike laminates that mimic natural materials like wood or stone, solid color laminates feature a uniform and consistent color throughout their surface. These laminates are preferred for their versatility and contemporary aesthetic. They are mostly used for countertops, cabinets, furniture surfaces, wall panels, and more!

Sparkle Laminate
Sparkle or glitter laminate, is a type of decorative laminate that features tiny sparkles, glitters, or metallic flakes into its surface. Such laminates are included to add a touch of shine, and visual interest to chosen interior surfaces and create an eye-catchy aesthetic effect. Sparkle laminates are often used in interior design and architecture to achieve a luxurious and trendy appearance.

Designer Laminate
Designer laminate refers to laminates that are specifically crafted to feature a unique and visually appealing aesthetic. Such laminates often include intricate and offbeat patterns, textures, colors, and even mimic the appearance of natural materials like wood, stone, or fabric. Due to their trendy touch and appearance, designer laminates are widely preferred in interior design and architecture to ace the visual appeal of chosen surfaces!

Mirror Laminate
Mirror laminate or mirror finish laminate, is a trendy type of laminate that includes a reflective surface resembling that of a mirror. Such laminates are designed to mimic the feel of actual mirrors while providing the practical usage and versatility of laminate materials. Mirror laminates are commonly used in interior design and architecture to provide visually appealing surfaces without the fragility of real glass mirrors.

Foil Laminate
Foil laminate or foil finish laminate, is a decorative laminate that features a thin layer of metallic or colored foil over its surface. This foil layer can mimic the look or appearance of popular materials like wood, metals, fabrics, and more. Foil laminate is widely preferred in interior design and furniture manufacturing to achieve a unique and visually appealing finish that mirrors the look of more expensive materials!

Colored Veneer Laminate
Colored veneer laminate is a type of laminate that blends the decorative appeal of the colored surfaces and texture and appearance of the real wood veneer. Such laminates are designed to provide the look of natural wood while offering a huge range of color options that may not be readily available in real wood. Colored veneer laminates make a trendy choice for interior design and architecture applications like wall panels, cabinetry, furniture, and more!

Rustic Laminate
Rustic laminate is a type of laminate that is crafted to mimic the look and feel of the rustic, lived-in, and weathered materials like aged wood, reclaimed barn boards, or textured surfaces that add a sense of history and character to the chosen surfaces. Rustic laminates are commonly used in interior design and architecture to achieve a warm and inviting feel to the classic, vintage or contemporary settings.

Designer’s Choice Laminate
Designer Choice Laminate are a line of laminate materials that are exclusively curated or selected by designers for their unique designs, quality, aesthetic appeal, and suitability for various design projects. Laminate manufacturers often collaborate with designers and experts to create laminate collections that cater to specific design styles, trends, or requirements.

Compact Laminate
Compact laminate is a sturdy laminate that is crafted from multiple layers of kraft paper and phenolic resin. It is preferred for high-stress applications and usages like wall claddings, tabletops, and work surfaces.

Laminate Finishes
Different laminate finishes determine the texture and appearance of the surface. The finish of a laminate can define the overall look and feel of the application. Some of the common laminate finishes are:

Gloss Finish
A gloss finish provides a shiny and reflective laminate surface. Such laminates add a smooth and polished appearance to the chosen surfaces. Gloss finish laminates are preferred for their trendy appeal and are widely used in modern and contemporary interior designs.

SF and Matte Finish
A matte finish is a non-reflective and low-sheen surface. Laminates with a matte finish provide a subtle and subdued look as compared to gloss finish ones. Plus, they are perfect to hide fingerprints and other surface imperfections too. You can select matte laminates to add an understated elegance to interiors!

Silky Matt Finish
Silky matte laminate is a type of laminate finish that features a smooth, satin-like texture with a low-gloss or matte appearance. Such trendy laminates are crafted to provide an elegant touch, soft sheen, and subtle shine to chosen surfaces like walls, furniture,cabinetry, and more. These laminates are used in interior design and architecture to achieve a modern, contemporary and sophisticated aesthetic!

Zero Matt Zero Dry Finish
Zero matt zero dry finish laminate includes the property of zero light reflection, meaning it does not reflect light. Zero finish laminates include dimensional stability at elevated temperatures and stain and wear and tear resistance. Available in various alluring patterns, colors, and patterns, zero matt zero dry finish are perfect for surfaces where general wear and tear, water spillage, stains are frequent and unavoidable!

Veneer Finish
Veneer finish laminate is a type of laminate that mimics the grains, colors, and textures of natural wood veneer. These decorative laminates are widely preferred to cover surfaces like furniture, cabinetry, and panels to get the look of solid wood at a reasonable cost!

Textured Finish
Textured laminates mimic the feel and appearance of different materials like natural wood, natural stones, fabrics, leather, and more. Laminates with textured finishes introduce depth, tactile appeal and character to the chosen surfaces in interiors.

Fluted Finish
Fluted finish laminate is a decorative laminate that features a textured surface crafted to resemble the look of fluted or ribbed patterns or designs. Fluting is an offbeat design that showcases the arrangement of parallel grooves or channels in a surface, mostly seen in architectural elements like columns, pilasters, or decorative panels. Fluted finish laminates are ideal to add depth, dimension, and visual interest to selective surfaces like walls, ceilings, furniture, cabinetry, and more!

PU and Deco Finish
PU and Deco finish laminate is crafted with a protective polyurethane coating (PU finish) and a decorative pattern or design (deco finish). Such laminates provide excellent durability, aesthetic appeal and make them suitable choices for a wider range of interior applications. 

Embossed Finish
Embossed laminates feature designs and patterns that appear to be raised above the surface and form a three-dimensional effect. You can select embossed finish laminates from different textures like floral motifs, geometric patterns and chosen custom designs. These laminates are ideal for including a unique touch to the furniture, walls, and other interior elements.

Suede Finish
Suede laminates have a soft, velvety appeal, similar to that of a textured suede fabric. Suede finish laminates add a sense of warmth and luxe appeal to chosen surfaces and are apt for luxurious interior applications.

Linen Finish
Linen finish laminates give the appearance of linen fabric and make a subtle and suitable choice for classic, modern and contemporary interiors. Linen finish is mostly preferred for beautifying the furniture, cabinetry and decorative elements.

High-Definition (HD) Finish
High-definition laminates are crafted with advanced printing technology to provide realistic and detailed designs and patterns. These laminates can mirror the look of popular materials like wood, metal, natural stone, etc with precision and exceptional artistry!

Sparkle or Metallic Finish
Sparkle or metallic laminates have metallic particles added as the decorative layer and create a shimmery or sparkly effect under light. These laminates are perfect to add a touch of glamor and a modern or contemporary vibe to interiors.

Chalkboard or Marker Board Finish
Chalkboard or marker board laminates have a surface that can be written on with chalk or dry-erase markers. Such laminates are mostly preferred for functional applications such as in schools, offices, or kitchens.The choice of laminate finish vastly depends on the required design, aesthetics, preferred application, and overall interior design style. Each finish provides a unique visual and tactile feel and allows the designers and homeowners to find the perfect match for their projects!

Leather Finish
Leather finish laminate is a decorative laminate that is crafted to mimic the texture and appearance of leather. These types of laminates are widely preferred in interior design and architecture to introduce a sense of warmth, luxury, glamor, and sophistication to selective surfaces.

Anti Finger Finish
Anti-finger or anti-fingerprint finish laminate is a type of laminate that is designed to resist fingerprint marks, stains, smudges, and other moisture-related marks. This type of laminate is often preferred in applications where frequent touching or handling is common like furniture surfaces, countertops, appliances, and other high-touch zones.
